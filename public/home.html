Small scale, big impact
At Acorn Wood, we know that children thrive in family-like, nurturing environments. Our small, carefully designed homes and our specialist day school provides the safety, connection, and consistency that our children need to heal, grow, and build confidence. By intentionally keeping our service small, we are able to prioritise each child as a unique individual, offering deeply personalised opportunities that support recovery, growth, and lifelong impact. Our approach is human-centred, not driven by efficiency or scale - focused on building strong relationships, supporting emotional wellbeing, fostering curiosity, and nurturing a lifelong love of learning. With relationships, empathy, and connection as our central priority, every child receives the care and guidance they need to heal, grow, explore, and thrive.
Education - embedded in nature and grounded in nurture
We believe passionately that human health and psychological well-being are supported by our relationships with the natural environment. At Acorn Wood we promote education through the inherent human inclination to affiliate with the natural world, by spending as much time as possible learning through, and with nature, whilst enjoying education through the great outdoors.

Why our colleagues love working at Acorn Wood
We prioritise relationships, empathy, and understanding, creating a workplace that mirrors the nurturing environments we provide for our children.
1.
Meaningful Work. Genuine Impact
At Acorn Wood, every day is an opportunity to make a lasting difference. Our caring approach means you are supporting children to heal, grow, and thrive - helping them build confidence, resilience, and a love of life and learning in safe, nurturing environments.
2.
Care for Those Who Care
At Acorn Wood, we believe that staff flourish when they are supported, valued, and guided. Our small-scale, nurturing settings provide a collaborative, reflective, and compassionate environment - giving you the space, training, and mentorship to grow professionally, whilst changing children’s lives.
3.
Professional growth
We are committed to the ongoing development of our team members. Through trauma-responsive and clinically-informed training, reflective practice, coaching and mentoring, colleagues are supported to grow their skills, deepen their understanding, and develop as confident, compassionate professionals - making a real, lasting difference in the lives of our children.
